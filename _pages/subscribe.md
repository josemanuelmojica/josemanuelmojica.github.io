Stay updated with new entries and reflections from this personal journal. Here are the ways you can follow along:

## RSS/Atom Feed

The most reliable way to stay updated is through the site's RSS feed, which is automatically generated by Jekyll:

**[📡 Subscribe to RSS Feed](/feed.xml)**

You can use any RSS reader application to subscribe to this feed and get notified when new content is published.

### Recommended RSS Readers

- **Desktop:** [NetNewsWire](https://netnewswire.com/) (Mac), [FeedReader](https://feedreader.com/) (Windows)
- **Mobile:** [Reeder](https://reederapp.com/) (iOS), [Feedly](https://feedly.com/) (Cross-platform)
- **Web-based:** [Feedly](https://feedly.com/), [The Old Reader](https://theoldreader.com/)

## GitHub Watch

Since this site is hosted on GitHub Pages, you can also "watch" the repository to get notifications when new content is committed:

**[👁️ Watch on GitHub](https://github.com/josemanuelmojica/josemanuelmojica.github.io)**

1. Visit the repository page
2. Click the "Watch" button
3. Choose your notification preferences

## Manual Check-ins

Of course, you can always bookmark this site and check back periodically. The [Timeline](/timeline/) page provides a chronological view of all recent entries across all sections.

## What You'll Receive

Subscribers will be notified of:

- New essay publications
- Daily daybook entries
- Letters and correspondence
- Any other journal entries or site updates

## Frequency

This journal doesn't follow a strict publishing schedule. Updates happen when there's something meaningful to share—sometimes daily, sometimes weekly, always when inspiration strikes.

## Privacy

- RSS feeds are completely anonymous—no tracking or data collection
- GitHub watching is managed by GitHub's own privacy policies
- This site doesn't use analytics or tracking scripts
